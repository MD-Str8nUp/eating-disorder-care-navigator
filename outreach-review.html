Website:
https://eating-disorder-care-navigator.vercel.app

The site is designed to help people understand when urgent medical assessment may be needed, how very low BMI and rapid weight loss can affect risk, what families can ask before leaving hospital, and how care coordination may help connect the GP, hospital, mental health and specialist supports.

Post 1 - 2026-07-12

Launch

I have created a free independent Australian navigation website for families and carers trying to work out where to start when an eating disorder may need medical assessment or coordinated care.

It includes urgent safety prompts, BMI risk context, a government BMI calculator link, questions to ask before discharge, and coordinator/contact pathways where public information is available.

It is not medical advice and it is not an official NEDC, PHN or hospital site.

Website:
https://eating-disorder-care-navigator.vercel.app

Post 2 - 2026-07-13

Medical Risk

An eating disorder can become medically unsafe before a person has had the "right" referral or the "right" appointment.

If someone is very underweight, losing weight quickly, fainting, unable to eat or drink safely, confused, weak, dehydrated, or physically deteriorating, urgent medical assessment may be needed.

The navigator gives plain-English prompts for what to ask at hospital or with a GP.

https://eating-disorder-care-navigator.vercel.app

Post 3 - 2026-07-14

BMI Context

BMI does not decide everything, but very low BMI should be taken seriously.

The navigator includes cautious wording around BMI 16 and below, and especially BMI 14 and below, because medical assessment or stabilisation may need to come before private therapy or ordinary referral pathways.

This is not medical advice. If there is immediate danger, call 000 or attend Emergency.

https://eating-disorder-care-navigator.vercel.app

Post 4 - 2026-07-15

Hospital Discharge

If someone with an eating disorder is sent home from hospital, families can ask:

- Is the eating disorder medical risk assessed, not just today's injury or issue?
- What should we do tonight?
- What symptoms mean return to Emergency?
- Who is responsible for follow-up?
- Is there a written plan?

The navigator includes a simple checklist.

https://eating-disorder-care-navigator.vercel.app

Post 5 - 2026-07-16

Coordinated Care

Families often get told to see a GP, therapist, dietitian, clinic, hospital, or mental health service, but no one explains who is coordinating it.

The navigator helps people look for the right care pathway and coordinator/contact point where public information is available.

https://eating-disorder-care-navigator.vercel.app

Post 6 - 2026-07-17

For Clinicians

For GPs, hospital staff, social workers and clinicians:

The navigator includes professional prompts for eating disorder medical risk, discharge planning, handover and care coordination.

Feedback or corrections from services and coordinators are welcome.

https://eating-disorder-care-navigator.vercel.app

Post 7 - 2026-07-18

Words Families Can Use

Sometimes it helps to have the words ready.

You can say:

"I am worried this eating disorder is becoming medically unsafe. Please assess the eating disorder medical risk, not just the immediate problem we came in with."

More prompts are on the navigator.

https://eating-disorder-care-navigator.vercel.app

Post 8 - 2026-07-19

Return To Emergency

If someone has been discharged and then becomes weaker, faint, confused, unable to eat or drink, medically unstable, at risk of self-harm, or otherwise unsafe, do not wait for an ordinary appointment.

Call 000 or return to Emergency if there is immediate danger.

The navigator includes practical questions to ask before and after discharge.

https://eating-disorder-care-navigator.vercel.app

Post 9 - 2026-07-20

For Practice Teams

GP practices are often the first place families turn when eating disorder care becomes confusing.

The navigator can be kept as a simple link for patients, carers and staff who need plain-English questions about medical safety, hospital escalation and care coordination.

It is not medical advice and does not replace clinical judgement.

https://eating-disorder-care-navigator.vercel.app

Post 10 - 2026-07-21

What To Ask The GP

Questions families can ask the GP:

- Is this medically safe?
- Do we need bloods, ECG or hospital assessment?
- Who is coordinating the eating disorder care?
- What symptoms mean we should go to Emergency?
- What is the next appointment and who is responsible?

More prompts:
https://eating-disorder-care-navigator.vercel.app

Post 11 - 2026-07-22

For Carers

Carers often become the people trying to connect the GP, hospital, mental health team, dietitian, psychologist and specialist service.

The navigator is built to help carers ask clearer questions and find the next responsible service where public pathway information is available.

https://eating-disorder-care-navigator.vercel.app

Post 14 - 2026-07-25

Why This Exists

This navigator exists because families can be left trying to work out whether they need a GP, a psychologist, a dietitian, a hospital, a specialist clinic, a coordinator, or all of them.

The aim is simple: clearer questions, clearer next steps, and fewer people left with disconnected referrals and no plan.
