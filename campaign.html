Marketing Agent plan

Zero-budget launch campaign.

A practical organic campaign to get the Eating Disorder Care Pathway Navigator in front of families, carers, clinicians and coordinators without paid advertising.

Budget: $0 Public site stays free Organic launch first Funding after proof of usefulness

Positioning

Independent Australian navigation aid.

For families, carers and professionals trying to work out the right next step when an eating disorder may need medical assessment, coordinated care, or escalation beyond ordinary referral pathways.

When an eating disorder feels medically unsafe, families should not be left with separate referrals and no plan.

Guardrails

Keep the trust clean.

  • Do not present the site as medical advice.
  • Do not imply affiliation with NEDC, PHNs, hospitals or government.
  • Do not charge vulnerable families for access.
  • Use the site to prove demand, then fund coordinator support.

Audiences

Who this needs to reach first.

Families and carers

People trying to understand whether urgent medical assessment, hospital escalation, or coordinator support may be needed.

Professionals

GPs, social workers, discharge planners, mental health clinicians, school counsellors and community support workers.

Future funders

PHNs, eating disorder services, foundations, clinics, philanthropists, carer organisations and rural health funders.

30-day plan

No paid ads, no paid tools, no paid PR.

Use organic reach, direct outreach, free community groups, existing professional networks and search-friendly content.

Week 1: Soft launch

  • Secure custom domain and verify HTTPS.
  • Add a simple support-this-project line when ready.
  • Prepare a one-page PDF flyer with QR code.
  • Post on LinkedIn and Facebook.
  • Send review/share request to 10 trusted contacts.

Week 2: Community distribution

  • Share in ED/carer support groups where rules allow.
  • Send flyer to GP practices and hospital social work teams.
  • Ask coordinators to check contact details.
  • Add two search-friendly FAQ sections.

Week 3: Professional credibility

  • Publish a short LinkedIn article about the navigation gap.
  • Invite feedback from PHNs and clinicians.
  • Add a visible last-checked date to contacts.
  • Draft a one-page grant/sponsorship note.

Week 4: Funding and partnership

  • Approach 5-10 potential supporters.
  • Offer paid professional resource pack later.
  • Start monthly impact tracking.
  • Keep family access free.

Launch copy

Ready-to-use organic posts.

LinkedIn

Families can be left trying to navigate eating disorder care at exactly the moment when the risk is highest.

I have launched an independent Australian navigation aid for people, families, carers and professionals trying to work out where to start when an eating disorder may need medical assessment or coordinated care.

It is not medical advice and it is not an official Right Care Right Place website. It is a practical pathway tool that helps people think about urgent medical risk, local coordinator contacts, hospital discharge questions and next steps.

The public site is free to use:
https://eating-disorder-care-navigator.vercel.app

Feedback from clinicians, carers, coordinators and services is very welcome.

#EatingDisorders #MentalHealth #Carers #HealthNavigation #AccessToCare

Facebook

I have built a free independent navigation website for families and carers trying to work out where to start when an eating disorder may need medical assessment or coordinated care.

It is written in plain English and includes urgent safety prompts, BMI risk context, a link to a government BMI calculator, questions to ask before discharge, and coordinator/contact pathways where public information is available.

It is not medical advice and it is not an official NEDC, PHN or hospital site.

Website:
https://eating-disorder-care-navigator.vercel.app

Funding path

Fund around the tool, not through families.

  • Start with $0 organic distribution until there is evidence of use.
  • Add a donation or sponsorship button later.
  • Apply for small grants for maintenance and coordinator advocacy.
  • Offer a sponsored professional resource pack with independence safeguards.
  • Consider paid professional webinars later, while keeping the public tool free.
