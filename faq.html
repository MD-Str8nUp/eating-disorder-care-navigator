Plain-English FAQ

When eating disorder care feels unclear.

Search-friendly answers families can share when they are trying to work out whether urgent medical review, hospital assessment, or coordinated eating disorder care is needed.

What should I do if BMI is very low?

BMI alone does not decide medical risk, but BMI around or under 16, and especially around or under 14, should be treated as a serious risk signal. If the person is fainting, weak, dehydrated, unable to eat or drink safely, losing weight quickly, confused, or medically unsafe, seek urgent medical assessment.

Should we wait for a private psychologist?

At very low BMI or where there are medical risk signs, a private psychologist or eating disorder clinic may not accept a person straight away, or may not be the right first step until medical assessment or stabilisation has been considered.

What can we say at hospital?

You can say: "I am worried this eating disorder is becoming medically unsafe. Please assess the eating disorder medical risk, not just the immediate injury or problem I came in with."

What should we ask before discharge?

  • What do we do tonight if eating or drinking is not safe?
  • When should we come back to hospital?
  • Who is responsible for follow-up?
  • How do we reach the eating disorder pathway or coordinator?
  • Has the GP, hospital, mental health team and eating disorder service been connected?
